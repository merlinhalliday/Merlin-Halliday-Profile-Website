Hi, I'm Djordje Atlialp.

my name is a bit unusual, but it just looks more complicated than it actually is.

My first name is basically pronounced like the English “George”. The surname is pronounced exactly as it is spelled.

I am Software & DevOps Engineer and Consultant and Technical Director of VIL GmbH . And I love to measure myself against new, exciting and challenging projects. I support the companies I work with both in implementing the projects technologically and in making the necessary strategic and tactical decisions to ensure long-term and sustainable product development.

A little history

It all began in 1991 in Bielefeld, my hometown. Shortly after I saw the light of day and the large facades of Sparrenberg Castle, I was introduced to the deepest and innermost secrets of the city in order to become part of the Bielefeld conspiracy . After exciting school days in Bielefeld, Bünde, Minden and Bielefeld again, during which I was also allowed to study chemistry as a young student at Bielefeld University, I wanted to do more with computers.

In 2012 I came to the Lemgo University of Applied Sciences (today TH OWL), where I completed my training as an IT specialist for application development. Due to the fact that my training took place at inIT, an affiliated institute for industrial information technology at the TH OWL, I was able to attend lectures in technical computer science as a guest auditor and provide proof of my achievements, which enormously deepened my professional education during the training. In the course of my training, I was able to develop a number of applications that can be classified under the keyword Industry 4.0. Most of them were used for process and control optimization in industrial production plants. Others to improve working conditions at manual workstations with the help of augmented reality applications.

software engineering

In 2015 I moved to Cologne, my adopted home, and then shifted my professional focus to backend development and solutions architecture. The central element of my research and employment was initially almost exclusively the area of ​​IoT (Internet of Things) . The requirements in this field are so varied that I constantly learned new methods, concepts and languages ​​in order to be able to develop the optimal solution. Every cloud solution is unique in function, scope and technical structure. To do this, I also worked with market leaders in their segment throughout Germany, such as well-known camera and household appliance manufacturers, as well as Boxine GmbH and StepStone.

In 2019 I'm mainly working in the field of enterprise e-commerce, which is as challenging as it is exciting. In addition to my work as a software engineer, I deal intensively with the topics of corporate transformation to domain-driven design and various types of organizational verticalization.

In 2020 and 2021 I worked as a freelancer on projects such as the digitization of administration (Bochum) and AI-controlled video analysis at RTL.

Managing directors

A new chapter in VIL GmbH began for me in September 2021, where I worked as technical director and managing director. Under my management, the company grew from 6 people to 32, introduced important instruments for corporate governance and responsible engineering and finally developed its product to market maturity. The product entered the market in May 2022. For a computer scientist, such a role is incredibly exciting because the nature of the problems are so different from those you already know. Thanks to intensive reflection and the advice of my colleagues, I have developed at least as much as the company itself. Probably the most instructive moment was the one in which I realized that good leadership means setting the course and giving up the wheel.

lecturer

Teaching and research are and always have been extremely important to me. And I didn't just see how much depends on the actual teacher when I was at school or university. One of my great role models from childhood, Prof. Dr. Harald Lesch was able to inspire thousands of people for physics and astronomy within a few minutes with a small table, a chalkboard and enough pathos ( alpha-Centauri ). His passion for the subject was so contagious that it didn't matter what the abstract was: it was just cool and super interesting. I would now also like to pass on the experience I have gained in recent years and my passion for computer science and the values ​​that go with it, and I am grateful that I now have this opportunity. theTechStarter GmbH is a company founded in Munich that trains people in second-chance education to become IT specialists for application development within a year .
